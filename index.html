Clinical Implications

  • NFGNB account for 42.8% of respiratory gram-negative isolates — a substantial burden requiring targeted empiric therapy
  • ICU predominance of NFGNB (55.8% ICU) vs Enterobacterales (52.6% ICU) implies different infection acquisition pathways
  • DTR rate is dramatically higher in NFGNB (55.8%) compared to Enterobacterales (27.0%), limiting treatment options
  • Species-level trends can inform local antibiogram updates and antimicrobial stewardship protocols
  • Data supports WHO BPPL 2024 prioritization of A. baumannii and P. aeruginosa as critical-priority pathogens
